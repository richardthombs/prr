## What Went Well

1. Strong contract discipline improved reliability.
   - Repeated enforcement of JSON-on-stdout and diagnostics-on-stderr appeared across stories and review remediation.

2. Determinism and composability became explicit engineering habits.
   - Diff/bundle and review/render pathways repeatedly added deterministic output tests and schema checks.

3. Cross-platform quality improved materially.
   - Epic 1.10 and 1.11 removed Unix-only lock/path assumptions and added Windows-safe behaviour and tests.

4. Review loops were effective.
   - Multiple stories show high/medium findings caught in AI-assisted review and fixed in-session before closure.

5. CI and smoke verification became stronger.
   - Epic 1.12 introduced cross-OS build/test/smoke checks with contract assertions.

## Challenges and Friction Patterns

1. Scope and command-model drift caused rework.
   - Story history shows pivoting between composable command surfaces and unified `review`/`render` model, including split stories (`1-9` and `1-9b`) and superseded planning artifacts.

2. Status consistency across artifacts lagged implementation reality.
   - `sprint-status.yaml` marks some story states as done while one related artifact remains `ready-for-dev`, creating traceability noise.

3. Foundational orchestration stories remain pending.
   - `1-2`, `1-3`, `1-4` are backlog while later stories progressed, which increases integration risk for end-to-end narrative consistency.

4. Quality issues clustered around the same theme.
   - Recurrent findings: missing flag parity (`--verbose`/`--what-if`), incomplete error classification, and contract/test gaps.
